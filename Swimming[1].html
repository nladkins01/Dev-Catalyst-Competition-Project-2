The Wonderful Sport of Competitive Swimming!

"If you say can't, you're restricting what you can do, or ever will do" -Michael Phelps

Swimming emerged as a competitive sport in the early 1800s in England. By 1837, the National Swimming Society was holding regular swimming competitions in artificial swimming pools built around London, England.

The fist Olympic games were held in 1896 in Athens, Greece and was a male-only competition. Six events were played for the swimming competition, but only four were actually contested: 100m, 500m, and 1200m freestyle and 100m for sailors.

The second Olympic games, held in Paris, France in 1900, featured 200m, 1000m, 4000m freestyle, 200m backstroke, and a 200m team race. There were two additional unusual swimming events, an obstacle course and an underwater swimming race. The 4000m freestyle was completed in only one hour.
